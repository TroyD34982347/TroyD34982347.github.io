Physiotherapy

Physiotherapy is a healthcare profession that focuses on maintaining, and restoring health through knowlage, physical help, disease prevention, and health understanding.

overall the most popular courses needed for all schools is Anatomy, physiology, statistics, and psychology.

University of Toronto

Admission Requirements

  • Required Grades: Minimum mid-B average in the final year of undergraduate studies.
  • Post-Secondary Prerequisites: Human Anatomy (0.5 FCE), Human Physiology (0.5 FCE), Life Sciences (1.0 FCE), Social Sciences/Humanities/Languages (1.0 FCE), Statistics/Research Methods (0.5 FCE)
  • Language Proficiency: If applicable, minimum TOEFL score of 100/120 (internet-based).
  • Other Requirements: Academic and professional reference letters, Health requirements, and Vulnerable Sector Screening.

Program Details

  • Location: St. George campus in Toronto.
  • Length: 24 months (6 sessions).
  • Curriculum: 18.75 FCEs including clinical internships and coursework.
  • Internships: A total of 30 weeks of full-time clinical internships.

Costs (Toronto Only)

  • Tuition: Approximately $24,000 CAD for Canadian students.
  • Books/Supplies: Estimated $1,500 CAD per year.
  • Living Expenses (Monthly): Rent: $1,200 CAD, Food: $600 CAD, Misc: $200 CAD.

University of Alberta

Admission Requirements

  • Required Grades: Minimum GPA of 3.0 in the most recent 60 credits.
  • Post-Secondary Prerequisites: Human Anatomy, Human Physiology, English, Statistics, and Psychology; 30 hours volunteer/paid work.
  • Other Requirements: Reference letter from supervisor.

Program Details

  • Location: Edmonton, Alberta.
  • Length: 24 months.

Costs (Approximate)

  • Tuition: $10,517 CAD for domestic students.
  • Books/Supplies: Estimated $2,500 CAD annually.

Additional Schools and International Options

  • McMaster University: Hamilton, Ontario, 24 months.
  • Western University: London, Ontario, 24 months.
  • University of Queensland (Australia): 2 years, Brisbane, Approx. AUD 35,000/year.

Professional Standards and Career Outlook

Title: Physiotherapist (PT)

Licensing: Must pass the Physiotherapy Competency Examination (PCE). Regulatory bodies include the College of Physiotherapists of Ontario and the College of Physiotherapists of Alberta.

Demand: High demand driven by an aging population and chronic conditions. Outlook is considered "Good" in both Alberta and Ontario.

Work Environment and Daily Tasks

Activities include assessing patient needs, developing treatment plans, manual therapy, and keeping patient records. Common settings include hospitals, clinics, and sports facilities.
